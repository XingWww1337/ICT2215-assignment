The sites that shaped three millennia of civilisation

Xi'an's landmarks are not mere tourist attractions — they are chapters of a story that spans three thousand years. Each site carries the weight of dynasties, the ambition of emperors, and the ingenuity of millions of nameless builders.

01

UNESCO World Heritage Site · 221 BCE

The Terracotta Army

兵马俑

In 1974, farmers digging a well near the Lishan mountain stumbled upon fragments of a clay figure — triggering one of the most significant archaeological discoveries of the twentieth century. What lay beneath the soil was an entire army, commissioned by China's first emperor, Qin Shi Huang, to guard his tomb in the afterlife.

Over 8,000 soldiers, 130 chariots, and 670 horses have been uncovered across three excavation pits. Every face is unique. Every figure was once painted in vivid colour. Scholars estimate that the full mausoleum complex may contain hundreds of thousands more artefacts still unexcavated.

Location Lintong District, 35 km east of Xi'an city centre
Best Time Weekday mornings to avoid crowds
Tip Hire a local guide — the context transforms the experience

02

Ming Dynasty · Built 1370 AD

The Ancient City Wall

西安城墙

Rising 12 metres high and stretching 13.74 kilometres around the old city, Xi'an city wall is one of the most complete and best-preserved ancient fortifications in the world. Unlike many historical sites that demand reverence from a distance, this one invites you to walk — or cycle — directly on top of it.

The wall was constructed atop the foundations of the Tang imperial city, making it a literal layering of dynasties. At its four cardinal gates stand watchtowers that have surveyed the city for over six centuries. As the sun sets, the entire structure glows warm amber against the fading sky.

Activity Rent a bicycle and cycle the full 13.7 km perimeter
Best Time One hour before sunset for golden-hour light
Entry South Gate (Yongning Gate) is the most scenic entrance

03

Tang Dynasty · Built 652 AD

Big Wild Goose Pagoda

大雁塔

Commissioned by Emperor Gaozong and built to house the Sanskrit sutras that the monk Xuanzang brought back from his legendary journey to India, the Big Wild Goose Pagoda stands as one of the finest examples of Tang dynasty Buddhist architecture in existence.

At 64 metres tall across seven storeys, it commands the skyline of southern Xi'an. The surrounding Da Ci'en Temple complex remains an active place of worship. Each evening, Asia's largest musical fountain display erupts in the vast square to its north — a spectacle that draws thousands of visitors.

Fountain Nightly show at the North Square from 8:30 PM
Climb Ascend all seven floors for a panoramic view of Xi'an
Nearby Tang West Market and Shaanxi History Museum within 3 km

Practical Info

Before You Go

✈️

Getting There

Xi'an Xianyang International Airport connects to major cities across China and select international routes. High-speed rail from Beijing takes 4.5 hours.

🗓

Best Season

Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) offer pleasant temperatures and clear skies. Summer is hot; winter is cold but crowd-free.

🍜

Local Food

Do not leave without trying Rou Jia Mo (Chinese burger), Yang Rou Pao Mo (lamb stew with bread), and cold Liang Pi noodles from the Muslim Quarter.

🗺

Getting Around

The city metro covers major landmarks. For the Terracotta Army, take Metro Line 9 directly from the city centre — no tour bus needed.
